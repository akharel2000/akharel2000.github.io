About

A bit about me

I'm a DPhil student in the Department of Statistics at the University of Oxford, working within the Computational Statistics and Machine Learning group. My research sits at the intersection of mathematics and modern AI.

I'm particularly interested in understanding the theoretical foundations of deep learning, developing principled approaches to causal inference, and investigating structured reasoning and security in foundation models.

Before starting my DPhil, I studied Mathematics at the undergraduate level with a focus on probability and stochastic processes, followed by an MSc in Mathematical Sciences at Oxford where I worked on stochastic analysis, machine learning, and diffusion model theory.
